A Sustainable Project

EcoLattice was founded in 2020 as a university project focused on developing substitutes for foam and traditional production processes. Since then, our search for sustainable materials from waste turned into a sustainable mission: to create lightweight, high-performing, and circular materials that stand the test of time. The project came into existence in 2023 when we started participating in design weeks internationally and soon established as a startup.

Contact Us
Mission

Our mission is to replace 30% of global PU foam demand by 2030. Our goal is to reduce waste, lower emissions, and establish a new standard for responsible materials.

Vision

Our vision is to improve health and well-being through customised material and design solutions.

Value

Curiosity sparks ideas. Commitment keeps them going, and Courage turns them into change.

Our Story

EcoLattice began not with a business plan, but with a beautiful mistake. What started as a 3D-printed textile experiment unexpectedly solidified into something new: a block of structural, responsive foam. That accident opened the door to a bigger question: could we rethink foam entirely?

“For me, materials aren't just what things are made of—they're how we shape the future. I'm on a mission to reinvent them until they do more with less. It's all about solving real problems with a little rebellion and a lot of curiosity.” - Yash Shah, Founder

Introducing EcoLattice to the global design community by unveiling our first automotive seat prototype.

Presenting our foams to an international design audience with a focus on sustainable innovation.

Gaining recognition for circular materials at Europe's largest design festival.

2024 - IKEA Foam Accelerator Winner

Selected as the winner of the prestigious IKEA Foam Accelerator, validating our vision and technology.

2024 - Raw Collaborative Exhibition, Ahmedabad, India

Displaying EcoLattice at a leading Indian design platform to connect with local innovators.

2024 - UAL Greenhouse Incubation, London

Joined UAL's Greenhouse hub and received incubation support to scale our impact.
