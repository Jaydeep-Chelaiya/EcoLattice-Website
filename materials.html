The Invisible Cost of Comfort

Traditional polyurethane (PU) foams, the industry standard for decades, are built from petrochemicals, bonded with adhesives, and are impossible to fully recycle. Once used within their short lifecycle, they contribute to an ever-growing global waste crisis. It drives us to innovate in the need for constant replacement and resource consumption.

Contact Us

Our
Sustainable Answer

EcoLattice emerges as a much-needed alternative to traditional PU, offering a material solution that not only provides long-lasting comfort but rewrites the script on responsibility. Our EcoLattice foam is not only recyclable but also reduces the need for toxic fire retardants thanks to its structural design and polymer stability. Its lightweight nature lowers shipping emissions and makes it easier to integrate into modular or flat-pack designs, further aligning with low-impact manufacturing and distribution strategies.

Efficiency

Our foams produce minimal environmental footprint by relying on natural resources.

Durability

Built from recycled elastomers, our foams are designed to last longer while maintaining their strength, comfort, and performance over time.

Circularity

Designed for a circular future, our foams produce no waste and can be completely recycled into new products.

Our Material Library

Khazana, Our Material Library, showcases a range of innovative, scalable alternatives to conventional foam.
This display brings together some of the promising innovations redefining comfort, performance, and sustainability in 2025 and beyond.

Banloop

Canvaloop's Banloop applies a closed-loop system to upcycle banana crop waste into high-quality textile fibres.

Malai

Malai produces bacterial cellulose composites made from coconut waste, offering a sustainable alternative to conventional materials.

Cane Weaving

The Wicker Story uses traditional Indian cane and rattan weaving to create intricate pieces for architecture and interiors.

Econit Wood

Econit Wood by Additive Tectonics combines sawmill and timber harvesting residues with a natural, non-toxic, and flame-retardant magnesium carbonate binder to create a sustainable composite material.

Mattr Leather

Mattr by Revoltech is a leather alternative made from algae, supported by a biodegradable substrate and enhanced with bio-based additives.

Recycled Plastic

Recycled Plastic Composites by Smile Plastics transform pre- and post-consumer waste—such as yogurt pots, chopping boards, and cosmetics packaging—into 100% recycled and recyclable architectural panels.

Rezign Vineer

Rezign Veneer by Planq is created from textile waste as well as bio-based fibres. The fibres are shredded, carded into felt, and pressed with a biodegradable starch-based binder to form hard veneer or 3D shapes for furniture applications.

Sole

Sole explores the intersection of herbal medicine and recyclable biomaterials, developing products such as gloves and packaging from aromatic herbs (Pogostemon cablin and Herba Eupatorii), aquafaba, seaweed, and flour.
