Importance of Sustainability in the Industry

The comfort we expect from everyday products comes at an invisible cost. Traditional polyurethane (PU) foams, the industry standard for decades, are built from petrochemicals, bonded with adhesives, and are impossible to fully recycle. Once used, they persist in landfills, release harmful off-gassing during their lifespan, and contribute to an ever-growing global waste crisis. Their short lifecycle, typically just a few years, makes matters worse, driving the need for constant replacement and resource consumption.

Sustainable
Materials

Polyurethane foam has become the silent backbone of our interiors. But what lies beneath the surface is a sobering environmental challenge. EcoLattice emerges as a much-needed alternative, offering a material solution that doesn't compromise on comfort but rewrites the script on responsibility. EcoLattice foam is not only recyclable but also reduces the need for toxic fire retardants thanks to its structural design and polymer stability. Its lightweight nature lowers shipping emissions and makes it easier to integrate into modular or flat-pack designs, further aligning with low-impact manufacturing and distribution strategies.

Our Material Library

The Foam Library showcases a range of innovative, scalable alternatives to conventional foam.
Tackling the global challenge of foam waste requires more than a single solution, and this display brings
together some of the promising materials redefining comfort, performance, and sustainability in 2025.

Banloop

Canvaloop's Banloop applies a closed-loop system to upcycle banana crop waste into high-quality textile fibres.

Malai

Malai produces bacterial cellulose composites made from coconut waste, offering a sustainable alternative to conventional materials.

Cane Weaving

The Wicker Story uses traditional Indian cane and rattan weaving to create intricate pieces for architecture and interiors.

Econit Wood

Econit Wood by Additive Tectonics combines sawmill and timber harvesting residues with a natural, non-toxic, and flame-retardant magnesium carbonate binder to create a sustainable composite material.

Mattr Leather

Mattr by Revoltech is a leather alternative made from algae, supported by a biodegradable substrate and enhanced with bio-based additives.

Recycled Plastic

Recycled Plastic Composites by Smile Plastics transform pre- and post-consumer waste—such as yoghurt pots, chopping boards, and cosmetics packaging—into 100% recycled and recyclable architectural panels.

Resign Vineer

Rezign Veneer by Planq is created from textile waste as well as bio-based fibres. The fibres are shredded, carded into felt, and pressed with a biodegradable starch based binder to form hard veneer or 3D shapes for furniture applications.

Sole

Sole explores the intersection of herbal medicine and recyclable biomaterials, developing products such as gloves and packaging from aromatic herbs (Pogostemon cablin and Herba Eupatorii), aquafaba, seaweed, and flour.
